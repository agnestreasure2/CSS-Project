Difference between padding. margin and border

Padding is the space between an element content and an element border, that is, if a content is touching the
border but you don't want that, you can use a padding. There is padding-top, padding-left
padding-right and padding-buttom depending on where you want the space but if you want an even space
you can use padding and give it a value

Margin is used to define a space around the element. Margin is the outermost layer of a border. For example
let's say we have 2 boxes but both boxes are touching each other but that's not the result you want, you want a
space between the two boxes, that's where our margin comes in , you can use either margin-top or margin-buttom for
either box 1 or box 2 and you will get the out come you are looking for. There is also margin-left and margin right
depending on how you want to style your work and there's margin: auto; for an even margin.

Border is used to set border on an element. A border has different properties, they are the border_style,
boder_radius and border-width which is used to size and beautify the border. An example is shown on my CSS file.
